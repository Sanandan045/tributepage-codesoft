Swami Vivekananda

Great Indian Monk and Philoshpher

Swami Vivekananda (/ˈswɑːmi ˌvɪveɪˈkɑːnəndə/; Bengali: [ʃami bibekanɔndo] (listen); 12 January 1863 – 4 July 1902), born Narendranath Datta (Bengali: [nɔrendronatʰ dɔto]), was an Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, author, religious teacher, and the chief disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna.[4][5] He was a key figure in the introduction of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world[6][7][8] and is credited with raising interfaith awareness, and bringing Hinduism to the status of a major world religion.[9]

    Swami Vivekananda - Contributions

  • Swami Vivekananda is credited with introducing the Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the West.
  • He dedicated his life to the field of social services.
  • His religious conscience was a synthesis of Sri Ramakrishna's spiritual teachings on Divine manifestation and his own internalization of Advaita Vedanta philosophy.
  • He addressed the people of India, urging them to abolish the caste system and promote science and industrialization.
